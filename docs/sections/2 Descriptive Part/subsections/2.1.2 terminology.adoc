=== 2.1.2 Terminology

==== Domain Concepts

*Activity* (Domain Concept)  
A physical action performed for fitness purposes, such as exercising,
running, lifting weights, or performing cardio.

*Exercise* (Domain Concept)  
A structured form of activity performed as part of a workout,
typically characterized by repetitions, duration, or intensity.

*Workout* (Domain Concept)  
A bounded period of time during which one or more exercises or
activities are performed with the intention of improving fitness.

*Workout Session* (Domain Concept)  
A specific occurrence of a workout that has just taken place at a
particular time and duration.

*Attendance Day* (Domain Concept)  
A calendar day on which a workout session has just occurred.

*Missed Day* (Domain Concept)  
A calendar day on which no workout session has occurred despite an
intention or expectation of activity.

*Goal* (Domain Concept)  
A desired level of activity or performance defined over a period of
time, such as completing a certain number of workouts in a week.

*Weekly Activity Pattern* (Domain Concept)  
The observable distribution of workout sessions across days in a week.

*Consistency* (Domain Concept)  
The degree to which workout sessions occur regularly over time.

*Streak* (Domain Concept)  
A sequence of consecutive attendance days with no intervening missed day.

*Progress* (Domain Concept)  
A change in activity patterns, performance, or consistency observed
over time.

*Workout Notes* (Domain Concept)  
Short written annotations describing what occurred during a workout
session or how it felt.

==== Domain Events

*Workout has just been performed* (Domain Event)  
A workout session has occurred at a specific time.

*Workout has just been skipped* (Domain Event)  
An expected attendance day has passed without a workout occurring.

*Goal period has just ended* (Domain Event)  
A time interval associated with a goal (for example, a week) has
completed.

*Streak has just been broken* (Domain Event)  
A missed day has occurred after one or more attendance days.

*Streak has just been extended* (Domain Event)  
A workout session has occurred on the day immediately following the
previous attendance day.

==== Domain Functions

recordWorkout(activity: Activity, duration: Time, date: Day) : WorkoutSession  
Creates a workout session representing an activity that has just occurred.

compareSessions(a: WorkoutSession, b: WorkoutSession) : Comparison  
Determines observable differences between two workout sessions,
such as duration or exercise composition.

evaluateConsistency(history: List WorkoutSession) : ConsistencyMeasure  
Calculates the regularity of activity over a time span.

computeStreak(history: List AttendanceDay) : StreakLength  
Determines the length of the current consecutive attendance sequence.

evaluateGoal(goal: Goal, history: List WorkoutSession) : GoalResult  
Determines whether a goal has been achieved within its period.

summarizeWeek(history: List WorkoutSession, goal: Goal) : WeeklySummary  
Produces an overview of activity, missed days, and goal achievement
for a completed week.

==== Requirement-Level Concepts

*User* (Requirement Concept)  
A person whose activities and goals are tracked by the system.

*Account* (Requirement Concept)  
A persistent identity that allows a userâ€™s workout history and goals
to be stored.

*Reminder* (Requirement Concept)  
A system-generated prompt intended to encourage activity when no
recent workout has occurred.

*Notification* (Requirement Concept)  
A message delivered by the system to inform a user about reminders,
streak status, or goal progress.

*Weekly Summary Table* (Requirement Concept)  
A structured representation of weekly activity and goal evaluation
produced for review.

==== Implementation-Level Terms

*Preset Entry* (Implementation Term)  
A predefined workout or activity configuration used to speed up logging.

*Quick Entry Workflow* (Implementation Term)  
A sequence of interface steps designed to minimize the effort required
to record a workout.

*Motivational Message* (Implementation Term)  
Text produced by the system to reinforce consistency or progress.
